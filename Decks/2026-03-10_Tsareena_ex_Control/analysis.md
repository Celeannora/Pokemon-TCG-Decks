# Tsareena ex Control — Analysis (Standard, Regulation Mark G+)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Archetype**: Tsareena ex Control (Tera Grass / Stage 2)
- **Win condition**: Use Icicle Sole to lock opponent's Active Pokémon at 30 HP, stack Toedscruel's Slime Mold Colony to cut off discard recovery, and force opponent into an unwinnable position where every attacker is perpetually on 30 HP.
- **Primary attacker**: Tsareena ex — PAR 46 (Reg G)
- **Secondary role**: Radiant Tsareena SIT 16 as a back-up Icicle Sole that gives single-prize pressure
- **Format legality**: All cards Regulation Mark G or later
- **Expected prize trade**: Opponent takes 2-prize KOs on Tsareena ex; you aim to take 6 prizes via Trop Kick + Icicle Sole finishes, ideally trading 2-2-2

---

## 2. Core Strategy

### Win Condition Loop
1. Get Tsareena ex Active with 2 Grass Energy
2. Use **Icicle Sole** [G] — put damage counters on any opponent Pokémon until it has 30 HP remaining
3. Boss's Orders that Pokémon Active next turn; Icicle Sole again to reset any healed damage, or finish with **Trop Kick** [G,G] for 180 damage
4. Toedscruel's **Slime Mold Colony** prevents discard pile recovery, meaning Hyper Potions, Super Rod, and similar healing recursion are shut off
5. Path to the Peak / Collapsed Stadium disrupt Ability-based engines while you control the pace

### Why Tsareena ex is a Control Win-Con
- Icicle Sole is a **guaranteed** damage placement to exactly 30 HP remaining — no flip, no condition, always succeeds
- Tsareena ex is a **Tera** Pokémon: safe on Bench from all attack damage
- 310 HP is among the highest in Standard, meaning opponent needs multiple attacks to KO
- Trop Kick self-heals 30 damage and clears all Special Conditions

## 5. Energy Line Analysis

- **Type requirements**: Icicle Sole = [G]; Trop Kick = [G,G]
- **Acceleration**: No built-in acceleration — attach 1 per turn. Control decks deliberately slow the game down, so manual attach is fine
- **Turn 2 attack feasibility**: T2 Icicle Sole requires Tsareena ex ready T2 (via Rare Candy) with 1 manual attach T1. Buddy-Buddy Poffin T1 onto Bounsweet, Rare Candy + evolve T2 is the optimal line
- **9 energy count**: Correct for a deck that rarely needs more than 2 energy per attacker and runs Super Rod recovery

## 6. Evolution Line Math

- **Line**: 3-3-3 Tsareena ex + 4 Rare Candy

- **Stage skipping**: Rare Candy lets you skip Steenee entirely. Steenee 3-of is a safety net only

## 8. Weaknesses and Mitigations

1. **Metal weakness (Tsareena ex is Metal-weak ×2)**: Tsareena ex takes double damage from Metal Pokémon. Mitigation: Keep Tsareena ex on Bench (Tera protection) until needed; rotate Shaymin Active as sacrifice if necessary
2. **Slow T1/T2 setup**: Stage 2 control is inherently slower. Mitigation: 4 Buddy-Buddy Poffin + 4 Rare Candy + 4 Ultra Ball + 3 Nest Ball maximizes T2 Tsareena ex odds
3. **No Special Energy / acceleration**: Pure Grass energy means no burst turns. Mitigation: Control game pace with Iono + Unfair Stamp so opponent can't set up their own burst
4. **Icicle Sole leaves target alive**: Opponent can still attack with the 30 HP Pokémon. Mitigation: Use Boss's Orders to pull a different Pokémon; or just Trop Kick the weakened target next turn
